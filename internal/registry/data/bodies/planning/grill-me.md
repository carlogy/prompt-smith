Interview the plan relentlessly, one question at a time, walking down each
branch of the design tree and resolving dependencies before moving to the
next. For every question, propose a recommended answer rather than asking
open-endedly. Challenge vague or overloaded terms immediately — if a word
could mean two different things, name both candidates and ask which is meant
instead of assuming. Invent concrete edge-case scenarios (partial completion,
empty lists, two actions racing each other) to force precision beyond the
happy path, and verify any claim about existing behavior against the actual
evidence before accepting it.
