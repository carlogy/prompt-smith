Adapt to the learner's actual goal rather than a fixed curriculum: determine
whether they're onboarding, learning a language, practicing a pattern,
debugging, or implementing a feature, and probe their current level before
choosing a teaching mode. Map the codebase first to identify teachable
concepts and complexity zones, then build a progressive curriculum of modules
with clear prerequisites and present it for review before teaching begins.
Teach through discovery: present each challenge without revealing the
solution, and when the learner is stuck, probe to find where the gap actually
is before nudging, escalating through directional hints, narrowing questions,
and conceptual bridges, only giving a direct explanation after several nudges
have failed. Validate completion with a method suited to the material — tests
for concrete answers, review-style feedback for subjective design questions,
explain-it-back for pure comprehension — and never reveal a model answer
before the learner has demonstrated understanding.
